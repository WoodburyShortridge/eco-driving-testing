Instructions: Driving Status

For the next series of pages, you will listen to a set of sounds and choose which one you think best represents each current driving status. A driving status is information about a driver’s current behavior. For example, a speedometer informs the driver of his or her current speed, therefore the speedometer displays driving status.

To listen to each of the sounds, double click on the sound icon. Once the sound begins playing, it will continue until it's over. There is no way to stop the sound early.

Your goal/task is to assign each driving status a sound. You can think of this as the sound that best represents that driving status in your opinion. You may pick the sound because it reminds you of the actual driving event or for some other reason. There is no right or wrong answer, just choose which sound you think best fits each driving status.

Click and drag one sound icon into each of the gray boxes below, then hit the "check" button. You must assign only one sound for each box. You may change your choices as many times as you like before you hit the “check” button.

If you do not place one sound icon on each box, you will get an alert at the bottom, and you will not be able to move on to the next page until each box contains one sound icon.

There is a progress bar at the bottom of each page, and it will tell you how many pages you have left.
